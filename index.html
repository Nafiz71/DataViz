Exploring Tech Job Market in Australia

The tech industry in Australia is experiencing rapid growth.

Key trends in salaries, job opportunities, and company preferences emerge.

This visualization provides insight into high-demand roles and regions.

See where the best opportunities lie in the fast-growing tech sector.

Salary Distribution Across Australian States

Salaries vary significantly across different regions of Australia.

Metropolitan areas like Sydney and Melbourne offer the highest tech salaries,

with many positions exceeding $120,000 AUD annually.

Regional areas show lower figures, though opportunities are still emerging.

Rural regions, while less lucrative, may offer a path for tech talent growth.

Estimated Salary Distribution by Tech Job Titles

Software Engineers and Data Scientists consistently lead in compensation,

earning over $130,000 AUD annually in many large firms.

Entry-level roles in technology begin around $80,000 AUD,

but these positions offer plenty of room for advancement.

As demand for tech expertise increases, these salaries will likely rise.

Job Title Frequencies in the Tech Market

The treemap visualization highlights the demand for various tech roles.

Software Engineers, Data Analysts, and Consultants dominate job postings.

Consultants are especially valuable as companies seek optimization strategies.

Over 30% of tech roles in 2023 were for these critical positions.

Data science and software development expertise remain high-demand skills.

So What are the Top Tech Companies to Get Hired By?

Australia is home to numerous tech companies offering competitive salaries.

Companies like Deloitte, EY, and CSIRO are great places to launch a career.

Multinational firms and local startups alike are driving innovation and growth.

In these companies, salaries often exceed $100,000 AUD for new hires.

So What Are The Highest Paying Tech Related Roles?

Software Engineers, Data Scientists, and Engineers top the salary charts,

with base salaries surpassing $120,000 AUD annually.

Data Analysts and Business Development Managers earn just below $100,000 AUD,

making these roles highly sought after in the Australian tech market.
